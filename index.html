Wenzhao Lian

I am currently a Professor at University of Chinese Academy of Sciences. My research focuses at the intersection of machine learning and robotics, particularly the following area:

  • Robotic manipulation problems including grasping and more dexterous operations (an old photo)
  • Imitation learning and reinforcement learning
  • Simulation-to-Real
  • Statistical inference, large models

    My research aims at artificial general intelligece embodied in the physical world in the long run, while solving real-world problems as we climb the mountain. Prior to the current role, I worked as the Director of Robot Manipulation at Figure AI, a staff research scientist at Google X , now known as Intrinsic, and a senior researcher at Vicarious. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Duke University in 2015, advised by Dr. Lawrence Carin. I received my Bachelor's Degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering in Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2011.

    I am looking for talented PhD students, master students, interns, postdocs, engineers, and collaborators who share similar interest and passion in robotics and AI (more details)! Feel free to directly email me at lianwenzhao{AT}gmail{DOT}com.
